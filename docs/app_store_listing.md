## FULL DESCRIPTION

---

**Take control of your money — starting today.**

budgr is the free, simple budgeting app built for teens and young adults who want to understand where their money goes, build smart financial habits, and feel confident about their future — no bank account required, no subscription, no sign-up.

**How it works:**

1. Enter your income — weekly, every two weeks, or monthly
2. Add your expenses by category
3. See instantly whether you're on track, tight, or overspending
4. Get a personalized financial game plan built around your actual numbers

**What makes budgr different:**

✦ **100% free, always.** No hidden fees, no premium tier, no ads.

✦ **No bank linking required.** Your financial data stays on your device. We never see it.

✦ **AI-powered advice, personalized to you.** budgr doesn't give you generic tips — it analyzes your income, expenses, and goals and gives you a specific action plan. Every recommendation is grounded in guidelines from the CFPB and FDIC Money Smart for Young Adults program.

✦ **Smart credit card guidance.** If you carry credit card debt, budgr identifies the 3 best payoff strategies for your specific situation — not a one-size-fits-all list.

✦ **Savings goal planner.** Pick a goal — emergency fund, car, vacation, home deposit — and see exactly how long it takes at weekly, biweekly, or monthly saving rates side by side.

✦ **Proposed budget plan.** After your analysis, budgr builds a line-by-line budget showing what to keep, what to reduce, and what to prioritize.

✦ **"What if I invested?" tool.** See how even $10/month could grow by age 65, using the S&P 500's historical average return.

**Built on trusted financial education:**
budgr's advice follows the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) Financial Well-Being Framework, the FDIC Money Smart for Young Adults curriculum, and JumpStart Coalition National Standards — the same frameworks used in schools and financial counseling programs across the United States.

**Who budgr is for:**
— Teens with their first job or allowance
— College students managing expenses for the first time
— Young adults building their financial foundation
— Anyone who wants a clear picture of their money without the complexity

Your money. Your future. Your plan.
